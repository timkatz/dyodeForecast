# DYODE 2026: Sustainable Growth & Stability Framework

**Created:** December 2025
**Purpose:** Chart path to long-term stability after 18 months of turbulence
**Approach:** Company-wide mission (NO individual incentives)

---

## The Problem We're Solving

### What Happened (2020-2025):

**The Boom (2020-2022):**
- Revenue grew from $2.6M → $4.86M (86% growth)
- Hired aggressively at premium salaries (pandemic bidding wars)
- Team scaled for $5M+ business
- Margins collapsed from 30% → 6-8%
- **Lesson: Fast growth without margin discipline = vulnerability**

**The Bust (2023-2025):**
- Revenue declined from $4.86M → $3.4M (30% decline)
- April 2024: Laid off 10 people
- Oct 2024: Gave raises (thought we were recovering)
- April 2025: Tariffs hit, business slowed
- 2025: Forecasted -$200k loss, froze raises, laid off 2 developers
- Year-end 2025: Avoided loss, ended at 0.3% margin (breakeven)
- **Lesson: External shocks + high fixed costs = crisis**

**What the Team Experienced:**
- Watched 12 colleagues lose jobs
- Got raises, then had them frozen
- Constant uncertainty and anxiety
- **They need stability, not another roller coaster**

---

## The New Philosophy: Sustainable Growth Over Fast Growth

### Core Principles:

**1. Profitability First**
- Revenue growth means nothing if margins collapse
- **Target: 12-15% profit margin sustained** (not just hit once)
- Never sacrifice margin for growth again

**2. Controlled Expenses**
- No hiring until margins support it (12%+ sustained for 2 quarters)
- Build in 20% expense buffer for external shocks
- Fixed costs should never exceed 85% of revenue

**3. Predictable Revenue**
- Focus on retention and recurring revenue (85% retainer / 15% project mix)
- Multi-service clients have 50-70% higher retention
- Quality clients > quantity of clients

**4. Team Stability**
- No more whiplash (layoffs → raises → freeze → layoffs)
- Transparent metrics so team knows where we stand
- Raises unlock at clear milestones (12-15% margin for 2 quarters)

**5. Risk Management**
- Assume external shocks will happen (tariffs, recessions, etc.)
- Build financial buffer (target: 3 months operating expenses in reserves)
- Conservative forecasting, not optimistic

## Expected Job Responsibilities (Not Optional, Not Individually Incentivized)

### Cross-Selling is Part of Client Management

**For PMs and Marketing Team:**

Cross-selling additional services to existing clients is **an expected part of your role**, not optional:

- **Identify opportunities:** When clients mention needs your other services solve
- **Make warm introductions:** Connect client to appropriate service lead
- **Support the pitch:** Stay involved to ensure smooth handoff
- **Track in CRM:** Log opportunities so co-founders can see activity

**This isn't about earning commissions. This is about doing your job well.**

Just like you're expected to:
- Respond to client emails promptly
- Deliver projects on time
- Attend QBRs
- Document client interactions

...you're also expected to:
- Identify cross-sell opportunities
- Ask happy clients for referrals
- Request Clutch reviews after successful projects

**Why?**
- Multi-service clients have 50-70% higher retention (your job is safer)
- Cross-sells grow company margin (unlocks your raises)
- Referrals are lowest CAC, highest quality leads (company stability)
- Reviews drive inbound leads (less dependency on paid channels)

## Financial Guardrails: Never Again

### Rule 1: No Hiring Until Margins Support It

**Trigger:** Can only hire when profit margin hits 12%+ for 2 consecutive quarters

**Why:** Prevents scaling expenses before revenue/margin can support it

**Exception:** Critical replacement hire with co-founder approval

---

### Rule 2: Maintain 20% Expense Buffer

**Rule:** Fixed expenses should never exceed 80% of quarterly revenue

**Example:**
- Q1 revenue: $950k → Max expenses: $760k
- If expenses are $900k → We're over buffer (danger zone)

**Why:** Leaves room for external shocks (tariffs, client losses)

---

### Rule 3: 85% Retainer / 15% Project Mix

**Rule:** At least 85% of revenue should be recurring (retainers), max 15% from projects

**Why:** Projects end naturally → churn. Retainers are predictable.

**Current state:** ~75% retainer / 25% project (too project-dependent)

**Track monthly:** Report retainer % at town halls

---

### Rule 4: Churn Must Stay Below 25%

**Rule:** Annual client churn rate cannot exceed 25%

**Current:** 35-40% (too high, unsustainable)

**Target:** 20-25% by end of 2026

**Why:** Can't outrun churn with new sales forever. Retention = stability.

---

### Rule 5: 3-Month Cash Reserve Target

**Rule:** Build up 3 months operating expenses in cash reserves

**Why:** If another crisis hits (tariffs, recession), we have runway without immediate layoffs

**Timeline:** Achieve by end of 2027 (build gradually from 12-15% margins)

## What Failure Looks Like (And How We Avoid It)

### Failure Scenario 1: Fast Growth, Collapsing Margins

**What it looks like:**
- Hit $4.5M revenue but profit margin drops to 5%
- Hired too fast, expenses ballooned
- Another 2021-2022 situation

**How we avoid it:**
- Financial Guardrail #1: No hiring until 12%+ margin sustained
- Focus on profitability first, growth second
- Conservative expense management

---

### Failure Scenario 2: External Shock Causes Another Crisis

**What it looks like:**
- Recession, tariffs, or client budget cuts hit
- Revenue drops 15-20%
- Another round of layoffs needed

**How we avoid it:**
- Financial Guardrail #2: 20% expense buffer
- Financial Guardrail #5: 3-month cash reserve (start building)
- Conservative forecasting (assume shocks will happen)
- Focus on retention (stable revenue base)

---

### Failure Scenario 3: Team Loses Faith, Morale Collapses

**What it looks like:**
- Team doesn't believe in another "plan"
- Top performers leave
- Execution suffers

**How we avoid it:**
- Transparent monthly metrics (trust through visibility)
- Acknowledge turbulence upfront (don't pretend it didn't happen)
- Deliver on promises (if we say raises unlock at 12%, they unlock at 12%)
- No more surprises or whiplash

---

### Failure Scenario 4: Can't Hit 12% Margin

**What it looks like:**
- Q1-Q4 2026 margins stay at 8-10%
- Raises don't unlock
- Team frustration grows

**How we avoid it:**
- Aggressive execution on growth plan (Martal, cross-sells, referrals)
- Ruthless expense control
- Focus on retention (stable revenue base)
- If we can't hit 12% by Q4 2026, reassess in Q1 2027 with team transparency
