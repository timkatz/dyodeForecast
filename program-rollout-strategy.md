# 2026 Growth Programs Rollout Strategy

**Created:** November 2025
**Purpose:** Coordinated rollout plan for cross-sell, referral, and review incentive programs

---

## Executive Summary

**Three Programs Launching Q1 2026:**

1. **Cross-Sell Incentive Program** (Account Managers)
   - 5% commission on contract term value
   - Target: 6-8 cross-sells = $40-58k MRR

2. **Client Referral Program** (Clients)
   - 5-10% of first contract in credits
   - Target: 5-8 referrals = $250-400k revenue

3. **Clutch Review Program** (Clients)
   - $500 credit per qualified review
   - Target: 10-15 reviews in 2026

**Why Coordinated Launch:**
- Account managers need training on all programs
- Client messaging needs to be clear and not overwhelming
- Timing matters for budget cycles and holidays

**Recommended Timeline:**
- **December 2025:** Internal announcement and training
- **January 2026:** Soft launch with select clients
- **February 2026:** Full launch to all clients

### **1. Cross-Sell Incentive Program (For AMs)**

**What it is:**
You'll earn **5% commission** on the contract value of any cross-sell you identify and close with your existing clients.

**Examples:**
- You cross-sell marketing to a dev client ‚Üí $7,500/month x 6-month contract = $2,250 commission
- You cross-sell consulting ‚Üí $10,000/month x 3-month contract = $1,500 commission
- You cross-sell development to a marketing client ‚Üí $8,000/month x 6-month contract = $2,400 commission

**How it works:**
- You identify a cross-sell opportunity with a client you manage
- You pitch the additional service
- Client signs contract
- You earn 5% (50% at signing, 50% after 90 days)

**Why this matters:**
90% of our clients only use ONE service. That's a massive opportunity. You know these clients best ‚Äî their challenges, goals, and needs. This is your chance to expand the relationship AND earn extra income.

**Important:** Not all clients are viable targets. We've identified clients who previously tried and stopped services (like Wyze, Tori Richard, United Scope). Check the past-service-history.md document before pitching. Focus on clean opportunities.

**Action:** By Dec 13, identify your top 3 cross-sell opportunities. We'll review together and strategize approach.

---

### **2. Client Referral Program (For Clients ‚Äî You Facilitate)**

**What it is:**
Clients who refer new business to DYODE earn **5-10% of the first contract value in service credits**.

**Examples:**
- Client refers brand ‚Üí $60k contract signed ‚Üí Client earns $4,800 credit
- Client refers brand ‚Üí $40k contract signed ‚Üí Client earns $2,800 credit

**Your role:**
- Mention the referral program in QBRs and check-ins
- Ask: "Do you know any brands struggling with their dev agency?"
- Track referrals in CRM (gives you credit for facilitation)
- Follow up when referrals convert

**Why this matters:**
Referrals have 60-80% close rates (vs. 30% for other channels) and close faster. Every referral you help facilitate makes your client happier AND brings in high-quality leads.

**Action:** By Dec 13, identify 5 clients most likely to refer (happy, well-connected, engaged).

---

### **3. Clutch Review Program (For Clients ‚Äî You Ask)**

**What it is:**
Clients who leave a qualified Clutch review earn a **$500 service credit**.

**Your role:**
- Ask happy clients to leave a Clutch review
- Send them the link and explain the incentive
- Follow up after they submit
- Track in CRM

**Why this matters:**
Clutch reviews drive 30-40% of our inbound leads. More reviews = more credibility = more closes. This is one of the easiest ways to drive growth.

**Action:** By Dec 13, identify 3-5 clients who would leave great reviews (happy, articulate, specific results).

## Conversation Scripts for Account Managers

### Script 1: Asking for Referrals (During QBR)

**Context:** Monthly or quarterly business review, after discussing performance

---

**Setup:**
"By the way, we just launched a referral program that I wanted to mention..."

**The Ask:**
"Do you know any eCommerce brands ‚Äî maybe in your network or industry ‚Äî that are struggling with their dev agency or looking for a new partner?

If you refer them and they sign a contract, we'll credit you **5-10% of their first contract value** toward your services. So for example, if they sign a $60k contract, you'd earn close to $5,000 in credits.

It's our way of saying thank you for connecting us with great brands."

**Listen for response:**

**If interested:**
"Great! Who comes to mind? Just give me their name and intro, and I'll handle the rest. You don't need to do any selling ‚Äî just make the connection."

**If they mention someone:**
"Perfect. Would you mind intro'ing us via email? Or I can reach out directly and mention you sent me. What works better?"

**If not sure:**
"No problem! Just keep it in mind. If anyone mentions they're looking for dev or marketing help, send them my way. Here's a one-pager with the details." [Send PDF]

---

### Script 2: Asking for Clutch Reviews (After Successful Deliverable)

**Context:** After launching a feature, completing a project milestone, or getting positive feedback

---

**Setup:**
"I'm really glad [feature/project/result] worked out well..."

**The Ask:**
"Would you be open to sharing your experience working with us on Clutch? It's one of the main places brands go to find agencies, and your review would help other companies make informed decisions.

To thank you for taking the time, we'll apply a **$500 credit** to your next invoice."

**Listen for response:**

**If yes:**
"Awesome, thank you! It takes about 10 minutes. I'll send you the Clutch link right after this call. Just make sure to include some specifics ‚Äî like [mention a result or experience] ‚Äî and honest feedback. Even constructive criticism is helpful.

Once it's live, just reply to my email and we'll apply the credit."

**If hesitant:**
"No pressure at all! If you'd prefer to do it later when you have time, totally fine. Just let me know."

**If they want to think about it:**
"Of course! Take your time. I'll send you the link and details via email, and you can do it whenever works for you."

---

### Script 3: Cross-Sell Pitch (Dev ‚Üí Marketing Example)

**Context:** Client has strong dev relationship, site is performing well

---

**Setup:**
"I noticed [website traffic has grown 30%] / [you launched several new features this quarter]. That's great momentum..."

**Identify the Gap:**
"Are you seeing similar lift in conversions and revenue? Or is there a gap between the traffic you're driving and what's actually converting?"

**Listen for response.**

**If they mention challenges (email performance, paid ads, etc.):**
"That's actually something our marketing team specializes in ‚Äî specifically for Shopify Plus brands like yours. We've helped clients like [similar brand] turn traffic into revenue with targeted email campaigns and paid social.

Would it be worth having a conversation about what that might look like for [their brand]? I can intro you to our marketing lead and we can put together a proposal."

**If they're interested:**
"Great. Let me set up a 30-minute call with [marketing lead]. No obligation ‚Äî just to explore what's possible and see if it makes sense."

**If not interested:**
"No worries! Just wanted to mention it since it's a natural next step. If it ever becomes a priority, let me know."

## Risk Factors & Mitigation

### Risk 1: AMs Don't Prioritize Programs

**Probability:** Medium
**Impact:** High

**Mitigation:**
- Make commissions meaningful ($5-10k potential)
- Track and report performance publicly (healthy competition)
- Recognize top performers monthly
- Tie to annual performance reviews

---

### Risk 2: Clients Feel "Sold To"

**Probability:** Low-Medium
**Impact:** Medium

**Mitigation:**
- Train AMs on natural, non-pushy approach
- Only ask happy clients
- Position as "helping their network" not "selling"
- Don't ask multiple times if they decline

---

### Risk 3: Program Complexity Confuses Clients

**Probability:** Low
**Impact:** Medium

**Mitigation:**
- Keep client emails simple (one-pagers, clear examples)
- AMs explain verbally in QBRs
- FAQ document available
- Personal follow-up if confusion

---

### Risk 4: Low Initial Participation

**Probability:** Medium
**Impact:** Medium

**Mitigation:**
- Start with soft launch (build momentum)
- Share early wins to motivate others
- Personal outreach vs. just email blasts
- Quarterly reminders keep it top-of-mind
