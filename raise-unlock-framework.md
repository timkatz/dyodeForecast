## Current Situation

**Salary Freeze in Effect:**
- No raises across all roles
- In place due to 3-year revenue decline and profit margin pressure
- Performance-based commissions available for client-facing roles as alternative

**Why the Freeze Matters:**
- 2025 projected revenue: $3.1-3.2M (down from $4.0M in 2024, $4.86M peak in 2022)
- Need to stabilize profitability before increasing fixed costs (salaries)
- Salaries are permanent cost increases; need sustainable profit margin first

## The Path to Unlock Raises

### Principle: Raises Unlock When Profit Margin is Healthy and Sustainable

**Key Metrics:**
1. **Profit Margin %** - Primary trigger
2. **MRR Growth** - Secondary indicator (shows momentum)
3. **Sustainability** - Must maintain for 2+ consecutive quarters

**Philosophy:**
- Growth alone doesn't unlock raises (we could grow revenue but remain unprofitable)
- **Profit margin health** is what allows sustainable salary increases
- Commission earnings in 2026 don't count against raise eligibility (everyone evaluated on total contribution)

## What Counts Toward Raises (Performance Evaluation)

### Everyone is Evaluated on Total Contribution (Not Just Commission Earnings)

**Client-Facing Roles (Who Can Earn Commissions):**
- Commission earnings in 2026 do NOT penalize you for raises
- Raises are based on:
  - Overall client management quality
  - Retention performance (did your clients stick?)
  - Team collaboration
  - Client satisfaction
  - Cross-sells, referrals, reviews (shows initiative)
  - Delivery quality

**Example:**
- PM earns $8k in commissions in 2026 from cross-sells
- Also maintains 95% client retention, delivers excellent projects
- When raises unlock, PM is evaluated on ALL contributions (not penalized for commission earnings)
- PM could get 4-5% raise on top of commissions earned

---

**Non-Client-Facing Roles (Who Can't Earn Commissions):**
- Raises are based on:
  - Team leadership and coaching effectiveness
  - Did your team hit their goals? (PMs cross-selling, devs delivering quality)
  - Operational improvements
  - Process efficiency
  - Strategic contributions
  - Cross-functional collaboration

**Example:**
- Director of Project Management coaches PMs to hit 6 cross-sells in 2026
- Improves project delivery processes, increases team efficiency
- When raises unlock, Director gets 4-5% merit raise based on team success and leadership

---

### Key Message: **Everyone Benefits from Company Success**

- Client-facing roles: Earn commissions in 2026 + raises when freeze lifts
- Non-client-facing roles: No commissions in 2026, but raises reflect total contributions when freeze lifts
- Directors already earn higher base salaries (so not getting commissions is balanced by higher pay)
- When raises unlock, performance is evaluated holistically (not just "who made commissions")

## FAQ for Team

**Q: If I earn commissions in 2026, will I get a smaller raise when the freeze lifts?**
A: No. Raises are performance-based on your total contribution, not penalized by commission earnings. You could earn $8k in commissions AND get a 5% raise.

**Q: What if I don't have a commission opportunity? Is that fair?**
A: Commission opportunities are tied to client-facing roles. Directors earn higher base salaries. When raises unlock, everyone is evaluated on their total contribution to company success (not just commissions).

**Q: What if we don't hit the profit margin target?**
A: We'll reassess and communicate a revised timeline. The 2026 plan is designed to hit targets. If we execute well, raises should unlock by end of 2026 or early 2027.

**Q: Can co-founders do selective raises before the freeze lifts?**
A: In rare cases (critical retention risk, promotion, role change), yes. But the general freeze remains until profit margin targets are hit.

**Q: Will directors get bigger raises since they didn't get commissions?**
A: Raises are merit-based on performance, not compensation for lack of commissions. Top performers (director or not) get higher raises. Average performers get standard raises.

**Q: How will performance be evaluated for raises?**
A: Holistically. Client-facing roles: retention, client satisfaction, delivery quality, initiative (cross-sells, referrals). Non-client-facing: team leadership, process improvements, strategic contributions.
