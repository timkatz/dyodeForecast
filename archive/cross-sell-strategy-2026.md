## Account Manager Incentive Program

### Program Overview

**Purpose:**
Reward account managers for driving cross-sell revenue during salary freeze period, creating win-win alignment between team compensation and company growth.

**Eligibility:**
Any account manager who identifies, proposes, and closes a cross-sell to an existing client they manage.

---

### Incentive Structure

#### Option A: Flat Commission Model (Recommended)

**Commission Rate:** 5% of contract term value (one-time payment)

**Example Payouts:**
- $5,000/month cross-sell x 6-month contract = $25,000 total ‚Üí $1,250 commission (5%)
- $7,500/month cross-sell x 6-month contract = $45,000 total ‚Üí $2,250 commission (5%)
- $7,500/month cross-sell x 3-month contract = $22,500 total ‚Üí $1,125 commission (5%)
- $10,000/month cross-sell x 6-month contract = $60,000 total ‚Üí $3,000 commission (5%)

**Why This Works Better:**
- Aligns with actual contract terms (typically 3-6 months, not 12)
- Fair to company: only pay commission on contracted revenue
- Fair to AM: higher MRR and longer contracts = higher commission
- Encourages AMs to push for 6-month vs 3-month terms

**Annual Budget:** $12,000 - $20,000 (for 8-10 cross-sells at typical 6-month terms)

**Payment Timing:** 50% upon contract signing, 50% after 90 days (ensures client success)

---

#### Option B: Tiered Bonus Model

**Tier Structure (based on contract term value):**

| Contract Value | Commission Rate | Example Payout (6mo term) |
|----------------|----------------|---------------------------|
| $15,000 - $29,999 | 4% | $600 - $1,200 |
| $30,000 - $49,999 | 5% | $1,500 - $2,500 |
| $50,000+ | 6% | $3,000+ |

**Annual Budget:** $14,000 - $24,000

**Benefit:** Encourages higher-value cross-sells and longer contract terms

---

#### Option C: Quarterly Contest Model

**Structure:**
- Top performer each quarter wins bonus
- Must have minimum 2 cross-sells to qualify
- Bonus pool: $2,500/quarter ($10k/year)

**Drawback:** Only rewards top performer, may demotivate others

---

### Recommended Program: Option A (Flat 5% Commission)

**Why:**
- Simple and transparent
- Rewards all successful cross-sells equally
- Aligns with actual contract terms (3-6 months)
- Encourages longer contract terms (6mo better than 3mo)
- Predictable budget impact
- Fair compensation for effort
- Aligns with "raise alternative" positioning

**Annual Investment:** ~$16,000 for 8 cross-sells at $6,500 avg MRR x 6-month terms
**Annual Return:** $52,000 new MRR ‚Üí Annualized value $624,000 if retained
**ROI:** 39x return on investment (first year)

**Commission Calculation:**
- Contract Value = Monthly MRR √ó Contract Term (in months)
- Commission = Contract Value √ó 5%
- Payment: 50% at signing, 50% after 90 days

## Program Requirements & Rules

### Qualifying Cross-Sells

**Must meet ALL criteria:**
1. ‚úÖ New service added to existing active client
2. ‚úÖ Minimum 3-month contract (no month-to-month for commissions)
3. ‚úÖ Account manager led the sales process (identified need, proposed solution, closed deal)
4. ‚úÖ Service launched and first invoice paid
5. ‚úÖ Founder pre-approves commission (to prevent conflicts)

**Does NOT qualify:**
- Client-initiated requests (unless AM actively sold/expanded scope)
- Founder-led cross-sells (unless AM partnership clearly documented)
- Renewals or expansions of existing services

---

### Process & Documentation

**Step 1: Identify Opportunity**
- Account manager identifies cross-sell potential during client work
- Documents client need/pain point in CRM

**Step 2: Get Pre-Approval**
- Submit proposal to Founder before client pitch
- Confirm commission eligibility and pricing
- Get approval to proceed

**Step 3: Pitch & Close**
- Present solution to client
- Negotiate terms and pricing
- Get signed contract/amendment

**Step 4: Commission Claim**
- Submit commission request with:
  - Signed contract/SOW
  - Client confirmation email
  - First invoice proof
- 50% paid upon contract signing
- 50% paid after 90-day success checkpoint

**Step 5: Success Checkpoint (90 days)**
- Client must remain active
- Services must be delivered successfully
- No major issues or complaints
- Final 50% commission paid

## Cross-Sell Playbook for Account Managers

### Identifying Opportunities

**Look for these signals:**
1. **Client mentions challenges** in adjacent areas
   - Dev client talks about poor email performance ‚Üí Marketing opportunity
   - Marketing client mentions site issues ‚Üí Development opportunity

2. **Client asks questions** outside current scope
   - "Do you do paid social?" = cross-sell signal
   - "Can you help us with X?" = opportunity

3. **Client success** with current service
   - Happy clients are most likely to expand
   - Strong results = credibility for new services

4. **Business growth/changes**
   - New funding, leadership, or strategic priorities
   - Expansion plans or new product launches

5. **Competitive pressure**
   - Seeing competitors pull ahead
   - Need to level up capabilities

---

### Conversation Starters

**For Development ‚Üí Marketing Cross-Sells:**

> "I've noticed your site traffic has grown 30% since we launched the new features. Are you seeing similar conversion rates, or is there an opportunity to optimize the funnel with email marketing or paid social?"

> "Your development roadmap is really strong. Have you thought about the go-to-market strategy for these new features? Our marketing team has helped similar brands drive adoption with targeted campaigns."

> "I see you're using [Klaviyo/Attentive/etc]. How's your email performance? Our marketing team specializes in revenue-driving campaigns for brands like yours."

**For Marketing ‚Üí Development Cross-Sells:**

> "Your campaigns are driving great traffic, but I noticed your site speed/mobile experience might be holding back conversions. Have you considered development support to optimize the customer experience?"

> "You mentioned wanting to launch [feature/product]. Our dev team has built similar solutions for [similar client]. Want me to put together a scope?"

> "The email campaigns are working great, but the landing page experience could be better. What if we optimized the entire funnel‚Äîboth marketing AND development?"

---

### Objection Handling

**"We don't have budget for another service"**
- "What if we started with a pilot‚Äî3 months at a smaller scope to prove ROI?"
- "Let's look at the cost of NOT solving this problem. What's it costing you in lost revenue?"
- "Would it help if we phased this in? Start small and scale as you see results?"

**"We already have someone handling that"**
- "That's great! Are they integrated with our work? Sometimes coordination between agencies/teams creates gaps. We could ensure everything works together seamlessly."
- "How's that working? If there are any gaps, we'd love to help fill them as an extension of what's already working."

**"Let me think about it"**
- "Of course! What would you need to see to make this a yes? Let me put together a specific proposal with ROI projections."
- "No problem. Can I schedule 30 minutes next week to walk through exactly how this would work?"

## Risk Factors & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Account Managers Too Busy with Delivery

**Probability:** High
**Impact:** Medium

**Mitigation:**
- Commission incentive offsets time investment
- Provide templates and playbooks to reduce effort
- Founder involvement in high-value opportunities
- Make cross-selling part of quarterly OKRs

---

### Risk 2: Cross-Sells Cannibalize Existing Service Budget

**Probability:** Low
**Impact:** Medium

**Mitigation:**
- Focus on incremental services, not replacements
- Ensure cross-sells solve NEW problems or needs
- Don't reduce existing service scope to "make room"
- Track carefully: are clients shifting budget or expanding it?

---

### Risk 3: Quality Issues with Rapid Cross-Sell Growth

**Probability:** Low-Medium
**Impact:** High (retention risk)

**Mitigation:**
- Maintain quality standards for all new services
- Dedicated onboarding for cross-sold services
- 90-day check-in required for commission payout
- Don't sacrifice delivery quality for revenue growth

---

### Risk 4: Commission Disputes or Gaming

**Probability:** Low
**Impact:** Medium

**Mitigation:**
- Clear qualifying criteria (3-month minimum, AM-led, etc.)
- Founder approval required before client pitch
- Documentation requirements (CRM notes, emails)
- Good faith interpretation of "who drove the sale"
